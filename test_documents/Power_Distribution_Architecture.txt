# Power Distribution Architecture - Project DC-2024-001

## 1. Electrical Capacity
The total utility feed capacity for DC-2024-001 is 20 MW, provided via dual redundant 33kV utility substations from the local grid. The facility operates at a PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness) target of 1.25.

## 2. Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS)
- **Topology**: 2N Block Redundant architecture. Each data hall has an "A" feed and a "B" feed.
- **UPS Units**: 8x Schneider Electric Galaxy VX series 1500kVA units.
- **Energy Storage**: Lithium-ion battery cabinets designed to provide 10 minutes of runtime at full 1500kVA load before requiring generator takeover.

## 3. Emergency Generators
- **Configuration**: N+1 configuration.
- **Engines**: 5x Caterpillar 3516E diesel generator sets, each rated at 2.5 MW (3125 kVA).
- **Fuel Storage**: Underground bulk fuel storage tanks containing 50,000 gallons of diesel, providing 48 hours of continuous full-load runtime without refueling. Refueling contracts guarantee fuel delivery within 8 hours of a declared utility emergency.

## 4. Power Distribution Units (PDUs)
- **Busways**: Overhead Starline track busways deployed above every rack row.
- **Rack Power**: Racks are provisioned with dual 3-phase 32A intelligent rack PDUs.
- **Monitoring**: All PDUs have environmental sensors and branch-circuit monitoring. Alerts are sent to the BMS (Building Management System) if current draw on any branch exceeds 80% of its rated capacity.
